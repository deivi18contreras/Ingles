Strategies to mitigate the negative impact of social media on student concentration and academic productivity.

View Analysis Give Feedback

1. Problem Identification

"The excessive use of social media during classes interferes with concentration and mental well-being."

The Context

In Moment 1, we identified that primarily High School and University students are affected. The constant checking of notifications creates a state of chronic distraction that impacts the whole social ecosystem, including teachers and families.

Who is Affected?

Students Educators Families

Why it Matters?

Decreased Academic Performance

Increased Procrastination

Poor Sleep Quality & Anxiety

2. Proposed Solutions

Educational Campaigns

Targeted awareness about digital well-being and the mechanics of social media addiction.

Screen Time Tools

Implementation of technology to monitor and restrict screen time during peak study hours.

Structured Routines

A phased approach to daily routines that prioritize 'Deep Focus' zones and device-free blocks.
